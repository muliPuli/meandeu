Introduction

Hi, I am Milan, the creator of the TESGO project.
The whole idea was to make a model car with self-driving capabilities from items that are widely available for teenage boys at home. The only necessity is the Mindstorms EV3, a computer with WI-FI and a smartphone. My model is a bit more complex, but it is only because I wanted to make it more portable.
The project itself took about 3 months to finish.
Most of the time was taken up by planning and researching, while the actual building and programming part accounted for only 25% of the time.

Details

The car houses several individual 'blocks' that communinicate with each other over a WI-FI router. The brain of the car is a Raspberry PI, that deals with object detection and steering prediction from the input array of images, using Tensorflow. The images are received from the server hosted by my smartphone. The outputs of the Neural Networks are then sent over to the EV3, that acts according to them.
